The primary indicator of hunger used by the UN Food and Agriculture Organization
is the prevalence of undernourishment as a percentage of the population.

According to Roser & Ritchie (2019), it calculates the proportion of the population
whose calorie consumption falls short of the minimal levels of energy considered

Stunting is a term used to describe the condition where a child under the age of 5 cannot reach their optimal physical size.

Sometimes, this is caused by rare conditions but this is also caused by lack of food and nutrition, in other words, hunger. This happens in underdeveloped countries or countries with a lot of misuse of government money (corruption, etc). But still most countries are working towards improving this as shown in the graph.

To understand how many children are suffering from a growth stunt, we must first see which countries suffer from lack of food and nutrition.

According to Roser & Ritchie (2019), the Global Hunger Index has been the primary measurement system to capture and track the process of hunger within a single metric. The index is calculated of 4 elements, undernourishment, Stunting, Wasting, and child mortality.

Putting the index against the stunting records in the scatter plot has shown us that there indeed is a correlation between hunger index and stunting. We can safely conclude that malnourishment is the main cause of stunting.

Africa and Asia have the highest number of countries with high hunger index and therefore has the most occurences of stunting.

Global Hunger Index

Hunger index's scale ranges from 0 to 100. They are classified in the following ways.

Extremely alarming : 50 or higher
Alarming: 35 to 50
Serious: 20 to 35
Moderate: 10 to 20
Low: below 10

According to Roser & Ritchie (2019), the overall number of persons who are considered to be seriously food insecure is shown in the charts. Around 697 million people experienced extreme food insecurity worldwide in 2018. Nearly 40% of individuals who experienced extreme food insecurity lived in Africa, and more than half were in Asia. The Americas, Europe, and Oceania each received 10% of the total.

Moving on to actions taken and progress, organisation such as the United Nations (UN) and World Food Programme (WFP), has taken action. As years pass the improvement can be seen, in 2018 data, we can see that there are little countries in Asia and South America with high hunger indexes. According to UN (2021), programmes such as food assistance from the WFP has taken place. This has reduced the global hunger index by incredible amounts.
